# constraint logic programming

an important extension of logic programming, so-called "constraint logic programming" (CLP)

## syntax and semantics of constraint logic programs

A constraint is a restriction or condition
- such conditions are typically formulated as atomic formulas
- our goal is to extend logic programs over a signature by constraints over of a sub-signature


constraint theory
- Let $(\Sigma , \Delta , \Sigma ^ { \prime } , \Delta ^ { \prime })$ be a constraint signature. If $CT \subseteq \mathcal{F}(\Sigma', \Delta', \mathcal{V})$ is satisfiable and only contains closed formulas, then $CT$ is called a constraint theory.
