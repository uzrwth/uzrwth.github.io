# preface

In chapter 1
- distance and related notions in graphs
- adjacency matrix of a graph
- trails, circuits, paths, and cycles treats several problems regarding Hamiltonian and Eulerian paths in graphs
- graphs with forbidden subgraphs

In chapter 2
- basic counting principles has been expanded
- binomial coefficients and multinomial coefficients
- pigeonhold principle
- Polya's theory, Bruijin's more general method of counting arrangements in presence of one symmetry group acting on the objects, and another acting on the set of allowed colors
- partitions, treatment of Eulerian numbers
- stable marriage
- combinatorial geometry
- Ramsey theory, Sylvester's regarding lines produced by a set of points in the plane
- the existence of convex polygons among finite sets of points in the plane

In chapter 3
- develops the theory of matchings further by investigating marriage problems on infinite sets
- a characterization of certain large infinite cardinals by using linear orderings
